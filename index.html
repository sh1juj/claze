The Process

From waste heat
to building material

A three stage thermal pathway that converts the industrial furnace's greatest liability into its most valuable co product.

Stage 01🔥

Harvest

Slag & Clinker Thermal Capture

Molten metal slag exits the furnace at 1,200 to 1,500°C. Rather than quenching this energy into oblivion, we capture residual heat at the point of discharge through proprietary heat exchange architecture.

Peak Capture Temp1,400°C
Stage 02→

Transfer

Controlled Heat Exchange

Captured thermal energy is routed through a closed loop transfer system to an adjacent calcination chamber. Zero combustion. Zero additional fuel. The heat that industry discards becomes the primary energy source.

Additional Fuel Required0
Stage 03⚡

Activation

Clay Calcination at 700 to 850°C

Raw kaolinite clay is thermally activated into metakaolin, a highly reactive supplementary cementitious material that can directly displace Portland cement clinker in concrete mixes.

Optimal Calcination800°C
